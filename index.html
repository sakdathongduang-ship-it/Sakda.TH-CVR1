Data Center Commissioning Process

5 Levels of Commissioning (CxL)

Data center commissioning is structured across 5 progressive levels, each acting as a mandatory gate before advancing to the next phase ‚Äî tracked and verified through color-coded tags applied to all equipment and systems.

CxL 1
üü• RED TAG Factory Testing & Site Delivery

Factory Acceptance Test & Equipment Delivery

Level 1 covers two sequential sub-phases ‚Äî factory-based testing before shipment (1A), followed by formal equipment receipt and material delivery verification upon arrival at site (1B).

1A
CxL 1A — FAT / Branch Test

Factory Acceptance Test conducted at the manufacturer’s facility or approved test bench. Verifies equipment performance, QA/QC compliance, control logic, protection settings, and functional integrity before release for shipping ‚Äî covering switchboards, UPS, generators, transformers, and PLC panels.

Factory Acceptance TestBranch / Bench TestQA/QC InspectionProtection SettingsControl Logic CheckFAT Report Sign-off
1B
CxL 1B — Site Delivery & Material Receipt

Formal receiving of equipment and materials delivered to site. Includes inspection against approved Material Delivery (MD) documents, quantity verification, condition checks for transit damage, and confirmation that all items match approved submittals prior to installation.

Material Delivery (MD)Equipment ReceivingQuantity VerificationDamage InspectionSubmittal ConformanceDelivery Documentation
CxL 2
üü® YELLOW TAG Cable Installation & Point-to-Point

Cable Installation, Tagging & Point-to-Point Test

Physical installation of all cable types ‚Äî power, control, signal, data, and fiber optics ‚Äî through conduit, cable tray, and ladder rack per approved drawings. Each cable is tagged with a unique identifier on both ends. Followed by systematic point-to-point verification of every cable run and termination against wiring diagrams and I/O schedules, confirming continuity, polarity, shielding, and termination quality at both panel and field ends before energization.

Energize and perform first start-up of each system ‚Äî verifying voltage, current, phase sequence, ECMS/BMS operation, and baseline alarm functionality. Includes Point-to-Graphic (P2G) verification: confirming that every field device, sensor, and I/O point is correctly represented and displaying accurate real-time values on the SCADA/BMS graphic screen, ensuring the control graphic matches the actual physical installation.
